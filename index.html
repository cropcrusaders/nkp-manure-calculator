Manure NPK Values

Understanding the nutrient content of different types of manure is essential for effective soil management. Below are the average percentages of Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), and Potassium (K) found in common types of manure:

Chicken Manure

  • Nitrogen (N): 1.5%
  • Phosphorus (P): 1.3%
  • Potassium (K): 0.8%

Cow Manure

  • Nitrogen (N): 0.6%
  • Phosphorus (P): 0.4%
  • Potassium (K): 0.5%

Horse Manure

  • Nitrogen (N): 0.7%
  • Phosphorus (P): 0.3%
  • Potassium (K): 0.6%

Pig Manure

  • Nitrogen (N): 0.7%
  • Phosphorus (P): 0.5%
  • Potassium (K): 0.4%

These values represent typical averages and are suitable for general calculations using our NPK calculator. They provide a reasonable estimate of the nutrient content based on the type and quantity of manure you input.

Factors Affecting Nutrient Content

It's important to note that the actual nutrient content of manure can vary based on several factors:

Animal Diet

The quality and composition of the animal's feed significantly affect nutrient levels in the manure. A nutrient-rich diet results in manure with higher NPK values.

Manure Age

Over time, nutrient content can decrease due to:

  • Volatilization: Loss of nitrogen to the atmosphere, especially in the form of ammonia.
  • Leaching: Nutrients being washed away by water.

Storage Conditions

How manure is stored impacts nutrient retention:

  • Exposure to Elements: Manure exposed to rain or sun can lose nutrients more quickly.
  • Containment: Proper storage facilities can minimize nutrient loss.

Bedding Material

The type and amount of bedding mixed with the manure can dilute nutrient concentrations:

  • High Absorbency Materials: Materials like straw or wood shavings can absorb nutrients, reducing the concentration in the manure.
  • Proportion of Bedding: A higher ratio of bedding to manure leads to lower overall nutrient content.
